Plot Summary

Set in the dystopian nation of Panem, The Hunger Games follows sixteen-year-old Katniss Everdeen, who lives in District 12, the poorest of the twelve districts controlled by the wealthy and oppressive Capitol. Every year, the Capitol holds a brutal televised event called The Hunger Games, in which one boy and one girl (called “tributes”) from each district are chosen by lottery to fight to the death in a controlled arena—until only one remains. When Katniss’s younger sister, Prim, is chosen as the female tribute, Katniss volunteers to take her place. She joins Peeta Mellark, the male tribute from District 12, who once saved her from starvation by giving her bread. They are taken to the Capitol, where they are trained, groomed, and paraded for entertainment. Their mentor, Haymitch Abernathy, a past winner and now a drunk, struggles to prepare them but eventually helps them understand how to survive—and how to gain sponsors, who can send gifts during the Games. During the televised interviews, Peeta unexpectedly declares his love for Katniss, which creates a dramatic "star-crossed lovers" angle that the Capitol audiences love. Although Katniss is unsure if it’s genuine or a strategy, she plays along to increase their chances of survival. The Games begin in a deadly arena filled with traps and dangers. Katniss uses her hunting skills to stay alive, forming a temporary alliance with Rue, a 12-year-old girl from District 11 who reminds her of Prim. Rue is eventually killed by another tribute, Marvel, which devastates Katniss. She honors Rue by decorating her body with flowers, an act of rebellion that sparks resistance in the districts. When the Gamemakers change the rules mid-game to allow two victors from the same district, Katniss finds a badly injured Peeta and nurses him back to health. Their growing bond—real or performed—continues to gain sympathy from the public. Together, they survive several deadly encounters, including an attack by genetically altered wolf-like creatures made from the bodies of dead tributes.

The Reaping

In District 12, Katniss Everdeen volunteers to take her sister Prim’s place in the Hunger Games after Prim’s name is drawn. Peeta Mellark is also chosen as the male tribute.

Journey to Capitol

Katniss and Peeta travel to the Capitol with Effie Trinket and Haymitch, their mentor. They begin to see how different life is outside their district.

Training & Interviews

The tributes train for the Games and display their skills. Katniss earns attention by shooting an arrow at the Gamemakers, and Peeta surprises everyone by confessing he has feelings for her in the interview.

Entering the Arena

The tributes are placed into the arena. Chaos erupts at the Cornucopia, and Katniss begins her fight for survival.

Surving the Games

Katniss forms a brief alliance with Rue. Rue’s death strengthens Katniss's anger toward the Capitol. The rules change, allowing two victors if they’re from the same district.

The finale

Katniss and Peeta face the final tributes. When the Capitol tries to reverse the two-victor rule, they threaten suicide by eating poisonous berries—forcing the Capitol to let them both win.

Returning Home

Katniss and Peeta return as victors, but President Snow is not pleased. Katniss begins to realize her actions have sparked rebellion.
